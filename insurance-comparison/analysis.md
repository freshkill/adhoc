## Key Takeaways

1. **All three Allstate options remain cheaper than AAA**, even Opt C with the added endorsements — savings range from ~$892 to ~$1,224/year combined.
2. **Opt C directly addresses the Water Backup / Service Lines coverage gap** flagged in the prior analysis: it adds $10,000 of Water Backup and $40,400 of Service Lines coverage (plus a Building Codes limit bump to $40,400) for a combined **+$246.93/year** versus Opt B — and it's still ~$892/yr cheaper than staying with AAA.
3. **Opt B vs. Opt A**: Bodily Injury Liability jumped from $100k/$300k to $250k/$300k for +$63.48 on the 6-month auto premium; homeowners premium also dropped slightly ($2,536.75 → $2,492.42), likely due to the All Other Perils deductible rising from 4% to 5% (matching Wind/Hail).
4. **Wind/Hail deductible is still 5% ($20,200) across all Allstate options**, vs. AAA's 3% ($11,850) — this remains the single biggest coverage trade-off and should still be the top question for the agent (ask to buy it down, and confirm Named Storm deductible, which is not shown on any Allstate proposal). Mitigating factor: dwelling/roof claims are paid at **Replacement Cost** (confirmed), so the high deductible is not further eroded by depreciation on roof payouts.
5. **Personal Property is still the one weak spot** — $121,200 at **Actual Cash Value** vs. AAA's $197,500. Dwelling/roof is replacement cost, but personal property (Cov. C) is still ACV on all three Allstate options. Worth asking for a higher limit and replacement-cost coverage, especially since Opt C shows Allstate is willing to adjust line items individually.
6. **Endorsement deductibles are not disclosed** on any proposal. Only All Other Perils and Wind/Hail deductibles are printed. Water Backup ($10,000 limit), Service Lines ($40,400), and Building Codes ($40,400) show limits but no deductible — confirm with the agent whether these carry their own (lower) deductibles or fall behind the 5% policy deductible. A $10,000 Water Backup limit is meaningless if it sits behind a $20,200 deductible.

Recommended next step: ask the agent for a quote that combines **Opt C's endorsements (Water Backup, Service Lines, Building Codes)** with **Opt A's lower All Other Perils deductible (4%)**, and clarify the Named Storm deductible — this would likely land between Opt A and Opt C in price while closing the biggest remaining coverage gaps.
